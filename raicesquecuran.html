About the campaign

Raíces Que Curan

Background: For centuries, Ecuador has been affected first by Spanish colonization. Currently, it is suffering the injustices of a second colonization: the appropriation of knowledge. The international pharmaceutical industry has been one of the great proliferators of this phenomenon to reach countries like ours and not effectively repay the communities that have helped them; or in other instances, try to patent medicinal plants to profit from them through the production and sale of medicines.

“Raices que curan” is a campaign that seeks to raise awareness on the part of Ecuadorians and foster a bioethical relationship between the Amazonian indigenous communities of Ecuador and the pharmaceutical industry. The campaign shows the importance of protecting ancestral knowledge and fair retribution towards communities so that there is a positive outcome among the community and the industry. The campagin is aimed at various audiences, both private and public, and formed strategic alliances in order to find the most appropriate way to reach the greatest number of people.

This campaign was so successful that the minister of education and environment offered us the opportunity to discuss the implementation of a patenting methodology and intellectual property laws for the Ecuadorian scholar curriculum.

Ticket for a fundrasing event. We partnered with Bassick, a electronic event company. They gave us a free venue and DJs to play electronic music mixed with traditional ecuadorian sounds. We recovered our investment, and were able to donate the rest of our proceeds (more than $500) to Amazon Watch, a non-profit organization that fights for indigenous rights.

We enlisted two influencers to promote our campaign events on their social media stories. Mirella Cesa (left), a famous ecuadorian musician with 223k followers, asked to be our influencer, as our campaign message resonated with her. She mixes traditional sounds with her music and uses traditional clothing mixed with modern styles. Raúl Molina (right) was our second influencer. He is a drummer from many Ecuadorian bands, such as Paola Navarrete, Guanaco MC, and Jazz The Roots. He has many followers despite Ecuador's music industry being so small.

Over the course of our campaign, 10 interviews were conducted: 8 over traditional radio, 1 over digital radio, and finally 1 in a newspaper article. In total, $5,121.26 were saved by using public relations strategies in the media.
